🚀 Getting Started

Yes, 100% free! No credit card, no account, no sign-up required. We generate revenue through ads displayed on the site, which means you never have to pay anything.

Simple! 1) Paste a direct image URL in the "Image URL" field. 2) Describe the animation you want in the prompt. 3) Choose your settings (duration, ratio, quality). 4) Click "Create Cinematic Video" and wait 2–5 minutes for your video!

Upload your image to Imgur.com (free, instant), Google Photos (right click → copy image address), Dropbox (shared link), or any image hosting service. Make sure the URL ends with .jpg, .png, or .webp for best results.

PixelMotion AI works on all modern browsers — Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge. It's also fully mobile-friendly and works great on iPhone, Android, and tablets.

🎬 Video Generation

Typically 2–5 minutes. HD quality with audio enabled may take up to 8 minutes during peak hours. You'll see live progress stages on the screen so you always know what's happening.

Be specific about motion, camera movement, and mood. Good example: "Slow cinematic zoom in, golden hour lighting, leaves gently swaying in breeze." Bad example: "Make it look cool." Include camera angles (close-up, aerial, tracking shot) for best results.

Use JPG or PNG, at least 720p resolution. 16:9 aspect ratio gives the best results. If your image isn't 16:9, the AI will crop it automatically. Clear, high-quality photos produce better videos than blurry or compressed images.

Audio generation adds realistic sound effects and ambient music that matches your video's mood. It takes slightly longer and costs more API credits on our end, but for you it's always free! Try it for nature scenes, action videos, or anything you want to feel immersive.

After generation, click the "Download HD Video" button. The video saves as an MP4 file to your device. On mobile, tap and hold the video player to save, or use the download button. Videos are typically available for 24–48 hours after creation.

🔧 Troubleshooting

1) Check that your image URL is publicly accessible (open it in a new tab). 2) Make sure your prompt is descriptive. 3) Try a different aspect ratio or duration. 4) If issues persist, wait a few minutes and try again — sometimes the API has temporary high demand.

If it's been more than 15 minutes, the request may have timed out. Note your Task ID from the result panel, then start a new generation. HD quality + audio requests take longer during peak hours (usually evening UTC).

Common issues: 1) The URL requires login to view (must be publicly accessible). 2) The image is too large (try compressing it first). 3) The URL doesn't point directly to an image file. Test by opening the URL in a private/incognito browser tab — if you can see the image without logging in, it should work.
